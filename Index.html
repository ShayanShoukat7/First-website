What is inside the ocean

The sonar picked up a heartbeat—miles below where anything should live. Something knocked back from the other side of the submersible hull. The lights went out, and when they flickered on, the diver was gone. We found a door at the bottom of the trench—and it was slightly open. The deeper we dove, the more human the screams from the radio became. My oxygen was fine—but I couldn't breathe, like something was pressing on my chest. The ocean floor shifted… and blinked. She swam out of the wreck we just discovered—wearing my missing brother’s face. Something followed us up… and it doesn’t cast a reflection. Our last photo showed an extra silhouette behind the crew—ten arms wide.
